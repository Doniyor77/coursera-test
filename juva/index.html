Juventus Football Club S.p.A. (from Latin iuventūs, "youth"; Italian pronunciation: colloquially known as Juve is a professional Italian association football club based in Turin, Piedmont, founded in 1897 as Sport-Club Juventus by a group of young Torinese students, among them was their first president Eugenio Canfari and his brother Enrico, author of the company's historical memory.

Gianluigi Buffon was born into an athletic family on January 28, 1978, in Carrara, Italy. He proved worth a record transfer fee by leading Juventus F.C. to four straight league titles.
